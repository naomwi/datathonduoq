## Main Answer
The `~600k OOF` vs `~883k public` gap is not only a model failure. A large part is caused by comparing different regimes and scales:

- Local OOF averages 2020, 2021, 2022 folds, where daily Revenue is mostly `2.86M-3.20M`.
- Public predictions for 2023-2024 have daily Revenue mean around `3.90M-4.00M`.
- In absolute MAE, a similar relative error becomes much larger when target scale is `25-40%` higher.

Normalized error tells the story:

- `long_2020_548d`: combined MAE `719760`, about `25.3%` normalized.
- `year_2021`: combined MAE `510754`, about `18.6%` normalized.
- `year_2022`: combined MAE `608433`, about `20.2%` normalized.
- Public best `883183` over best-submission Revenue mean `~3.998M` is about `22.1%`.
- A `700k` public score would be about `17.5%`.

So the top teams are likely not `200k` better in the same OOF regime; they are about `4-5 percentage points` better in relative error on a high-scale future regime.

## Why Local OOF Is Too Optimistic

### 1. Fold Averaging Hides Hard Regimes
The old headline OOF around `600k` averages easy and hard years.

Shifted backtests:

- `year_2020`: combined `656327`.
- `year_2021`: combined `510754`.
- `year_2022`: combined `608433`.
- `long_2020_548d`: combined `719761`.
- `long_2021_548d`: combined `592665`.

Public is a 548-day forward period, not a 365-day fold. The 548-day 2020 fold is much closer to public difficulty than the average fold.

### 2. Public Period Is a Higher-Revenue Regime
Current public anchor predicts:

- `public_all` Revenue mean: `3.895886M`.
- `public_2023` Revenue mean: `3.522274M`.
- `public_2024_h1` Revenue mean: `4.641067M`.

Training actual annual Revenue means:

- 2020: `2.881181M`.
- 2021: `2.857643M`.
- 2022: `3.204791M`.

This is a large scale shift. Absolute MAE naturally grows.

### 3. Future Raw Features Stop at 2022
Raw future observations do not exist after 2022:

- sales/orders/promotions/inventory/web traffic all stop at 2022.
- 2023-2024 features are generated by future policies and recursive history.

This means the winning approach must reconstruct future calendar/level from priors, not rely on observed future orders/traffic.

### 4. Promo Policy Is the Only Confirmed Public Lever
Public probe evidence:

- Anchor: about `896k`.
- Flat promo Revenue +8%: `887225.99926`.
- TabPFN windowmix: `883183.19507`.
- True TabPFN 2.6 windowmix: `883416.15633`.
- TabPFN 2.6 scale105: `883183.43978`.

The public signal is main promo-window Revenue shape. That is real.

But extra Urban promo was rejected:

- `submission_public_parity_urban23_nonmain_up10_only.csv = 884654.02008`.

So hidden odd-year Urban is an OOF trap, not the missing public key.

### 5. Price/Context Features Are Validation Traps
`price_history` looked excellent offline but scored `923770.94996` public.

Reason:

- price/context/order/traffic/inventory dimensions are unknown in 2023-2024.
- future policy imputation can look good in 2020-2022 but fail in public.
- broad price/history submissions distorted both Revenue and COGS in the wrong direction.

Future policy summary:

- promo policy `recent_2y` matches recent promo calendar reasonably in main windows.
- price policy reconstructs avg unit price/margin but public rejected its usage as a direct model feature.
- context policy is risky unless carefully policy-filled and validated with fixed public anchor.

### 6. COVID Matters, But Indirectly
COVID/regime shift is real:

- 2020 Revenue mean `2.88M`, COGS ratio `0.847`.
- 2021 Revenue mean `2.86M`, COGS ratio `0.921`.
- 2022 Revenue mean `3.20M`, COGS ratio `0.879`.

But public did not reward broad recovery or broad COGS moves:

- `rev2024h1_up5` was only around `896k`.
- `cogs2024h1_floor87` scored `898472.39191`.

So COVID is mainly why validation is unstable, not a direct knob.

## What Top Teams Probably Have
To get near `700k`, they likely improved relative error from about `22%` to `17-18%`, not just changed model.

Most plausible sources:

1. Better future level calibration for the high-scale 2023-2024 regime.
2. Better main promo-window shape/calendar.
3. Better COGS/Revenue ratio handling during promo periods without broad COGS floors.
4. A validation scheme closer to public: 548-day forward, high-scale weighted, fixed anchor, normalized metrics.
5. Possibly target decomposition such as order volume x AOV, if they can forecast AOV/volume separately without leaking.

## Next Technical Direction
Stop treating average 2020-2022 OOF as the gate.

Use a public-like validation score:

- 548-day forward horizon.
- normalized MAE / WAPE-like metrics.
- worst-case or high-scale weighted folds.
- fixed anchor `submission_catboost_md2y_core_recencyexp20.csv`.
- no dynamic offline winner as anchor.

The next experiments should target:

1. Main promo-window shape refinement.
2. Promo COGS co-move at tiny levels only after Revenue improves.
3. Level calibration by normalized fold, not broad 2024 uplift.
4. Decomposition donor for order_count/AOV only if it wins 548-day hard folds.
